# DIAL

A design exploration reimagining a vintage JVC boombox as a modern **time-travel music discovery interface**.

Instead of browsing playlists, listeners spin a decade dial to travel through time and explore music from specific eras and places.

Live Demo  
https://mikchick.github.io/boombox-music-player/

---

## Concept

Music discovery today is mostly flat — endless lists, algorithmic feeds, and playlists.

DIAL explores a different model:

**What if discovering music felt like traveling through time?**

Users navigate decades like destinations and open "portals" into musical moments — cities, genres, and cultural scenes that shaped music history.

The interface blends:

• vintage hardware metaphors  
• modern streaming UX  
• editorial storytelling

---

## Core Interaction Model

### The Dial

The central interaction is a **decade dial** inspired by analog boombox hardware.

Users spin through time:

Each decade reveals potential portals into music history.

---

### Portals

Opening a portal transports the listener to a specific **time + place + genre**.

Examples:

1991 — Seattle  
Grunge erupts from the Pacific Northwest.  
Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Soundgarden redefine rock.

1977 — London  
Punk explodes across the UK underground scene.

1979 — Tokyo  
Electronic and synth-pop begin shaping Japanese pop culture.

Each portal becomes a **musical time capsule**.

---

## Experience Flow

1. Onboarding  
Users select artists and genres to tune their "time machine".

2. Era Selection  
The dial lets users explore different decades.

3. Portal Discovery  
Recommended portals appear based on taste.

4. Listening Session  
Opening a portal reveals a track, artist context, and related music.

## Design Goals

This project explores several UX ideas:

**Spatial music discovery**  
Navigating music by time and place rather than lists.

**Physical interaction metaphors**  
Using hardware-inspired controls to create intuitive digital interfaces.

**Editorial storytelling in streaming**  
Reintroducing cultural context to music discovery.
